Update to keep your membership Migration of Solr to Opensearch Migration of 100 million document from solr to opensearch Abhishek Mishra Abhishek Mishra Follow 2 min read · May 5, 2023 17 Listen Share More We used solr for our e-commerce search/feed/autosuggest/personalisation use cases. /Challenges with solr cluster/ * Managing solr infrastructure (zookeeper , JVM, GC analysis etc.) is a challenge. In general, we need a dedicated developer(SRE/SDE-3) for it. * Upgradation to a newer stable version. Less community support for any critical issues. Quick Comparison Elasticsearch vs Solr Press enter or click to view image in full size ELK now comes with elastic premium license. So we opted for opensearch as it is opensource. It is based on elasticsearch 7.10 version. It has apache license. Major Challenges * Existing code had design problems. Tightly coupled with solr. Microservice architecture and multiple service dependency. * Big cluster size in some regions & country-wise launches. Solution 1. We used terraform for cluster formation of opensearch in all regions. 2. For code we used factory design pattern and Adapter design pattern. 3. Parallel data ingestion in both opensearch & solr. 4. Migration of delta data opensearch & solr by our reindexing service. 5. One by one service launch with the support for both opensearch & solr 6. Once the steps 1 to 5 was ready. We planned country wise launch and monitoring on newRelic. Approx. architecture Press enter or click to view image in full size *Major bottleneck*: It was around the choice of node type c5 AWS machine. We fixed it by benchmarking and chose to go with c6g. Result We successfully migrated over 100 million documents from solr to opensearch with no downtime. Migration involved over 100+ nodes on solr and opensearch. We also managed to save approximately 0.8 million USD. Reference 1. Factory design pattern 2. Terraform 3. Opensearch 4. Solr 5.
